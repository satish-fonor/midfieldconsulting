Core services

Work focused on making internal AI tools more reliable, more maintainable, and more useful in day-to-day operations.

Prototype review and triage

We look at what you built, where it breaks, and what the shortest realistic path forward looks like.

Workflow cleanup

We simplify brittle logic, remove unnecessary complexity, and turn patchwork steps into a clearer operating flow.

Integration planning and implementation

We help the tool connect to the systems your business actually uses, including email, documents, CRMs, spreadsheets, and internal data.

Data and output quality fixes

We focus on the inputs, validation points, and failure paths that create the most friction for your team.

Deployment and operating readiness

We help make the software easier to run, easier to update, and less dependent on one person knowing all the hidden details.

Build direction and prioritization

When the next move is unclear, we help you decide whether to patch, rebuild, narrow scope, or pause.

Common engagement types

Most projects fall into one of these categories.

Audit and decision support

Useful when you are unsure whether the current build is worth repairing or where the real blocker actually is.

  • Review the current setup
  • Identify structural risks
  • Recommend the next practical move

Cleanup and hardening

Useful when the prototype already proves value, but real-world usage exposes reliability and maintenance problems.

  • Fix fragile workflow paths
  • Improve data handling and handoffs
  • Reduce manual rescue work

Focused rebuild support

Useful when the current version taught you what matters, but the structure now needs a cleaner foundation.

  • Keep what is worth saving
  • Reduce complexity before rebuilding
  • Prioritize the critical path

Founder-side technical guidance

Useful when you need a strong technical second opinion without hiring a full team or starting a long consulting process.

  • Plain-language technical judgment
  • Tradeoff review
  • Support through early decisions

What you should expect

Direct communication, practical tradeoffs, and a bias toward clarity.

No generic AI strategy language

The work stays tied to the workflow, software, and operating problems in front of you.

No pressure to overbuild

If the smallest useful fix is the right answer, that is the answer we aim for.

No handoff theater

The goal is a system and plan your team can understand and continue using with confidence.
