The first version is exciting. Then the real business shows up.

It worked in a demo, then failed on real data

Inputs are inconsistent, documents are messy, edge cases pile up, and nobody knows what the tool will do next.

  • Bad extractions
  • Wrong answers
  • Manual cleanup after every run

The workflow depends on one person who understands the hacks

Prompts, spreadsheets, scripts, and APIs are wired together just well enough to work until the next change lands.

  • No clean handoff
  • No clear ownership
  • No confidence in changes

Deployment and integrations become the real bottleneck

  • CRM or ERP write-backs
  • Email and document flows

You are not sure whether to patch, rebuild, or stop

What started as a quick experiment now needs a real product decision, not more random iteration.
