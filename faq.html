Frequently asked questions

Direct answers about scope, fit, and how the first engagement works.

Who is Midfield a good fit for?

Midfield is a good fit for founders, operators, and hands-on builders who already created an internal AI tool, workflow, or automation and now need help making it more dependable.

Do I need a finished product before reaching out?

No. A rough internal app, automation, chatbot, or dashboard is enough as long as there is something concrete to review.

What if I am not sure whether to patch the current version or rebuild?

That is one of the main reasons to book a consultation. The goal is to help you decide whether the current build is worth keeping and what should happen next.

Do you only advise, or do you also help implement?

Both. The first step is often advisory so the direction is clear. If there is a good case for further work, Midfield can help with focused cleanup, repair, or follow-on implementation.

What kinds of problems do you usually help with?

Most of the work centers on messy data, unreliable outputs, brittle architecture, awkward integrations, deployment issues, and uncertainty about what the product should become.

How does the paid consultation work?

You share the tool or workflow, describe where it is getting stuck, and use the call to review the build, discuss tradeoffs, and define the next practical step.

What if the consultation is not useful?

The consultation is satisfaction guaranteed. If it does not give you useful clarity, Midfield will make it right.

Do you work like a big consulting firm?

No. This is a small, founder-led practice. The point is direct technical judgment and practical help, not a layered consulting process.
